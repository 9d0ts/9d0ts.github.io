Augusta Ada King, Countess of Lovelace

Augusta Ada King (1815-1852), known more familiarly as Ada Lovelace, was the daughter of the famous British poet, Lord George Gordon Byron.

Ada met Charles Babbage in 1832, when she was 17 years old. He encouraged her to study mathematics. Eventually, she translated an article about Babbage's analytical engine from French to English, and in the process she became fascinated by the machine. She added her own notes to the translation, and they became 3 times longer than the article itself. The notes described how to create codes to represent symbols and letters as well as numbers. They also described how the engine could repeat a list of instructions, a process called "looping" that modern computers still use. For these reasons, Lovelace is often called the first computer programmer.
