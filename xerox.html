Xerox Alto

One of the most interesting stories in computer history concerns a company that doesn't make computers anymore. In 1973, Xerox Corporation pioneered a radically new computer called Alto. This computer had the first mouse and graphical user interface (GUI), allowing users to move a pointer and click the screen to indicate what they wanted to do instead of typing commands.

The Alto eventually revolutionized personal computing, but Xerox never made a penny from it. Instead, in 1979 a young entrepreneur named Steve Jobs heard about Alto and visited Xerox. He was stunned by what he saw and recognized its potential. He went back to his own company, Apple, and developed the Macintosh, which became first popular personal computer with a GUI and a mouse. Years before Microsoft, Apple launched the future of personal computing.
