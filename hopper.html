The First Computer Bug

Grace Hopper (1906 – 1992) was a computer scientist and also a Rear Admiral in the United States Navy. She was one of the first computer programmers, and she pioneered the development of programming languages that could work across many different computers. (Until then, each computer had its own language.)

On September 9, 1945, at 3:45 p.m., Grace Hopper recorded an event that made her famous. While investigating a problem in her computer program, she found a surprising cause: a moth was stuck among the components of her Harvard Mark II computer. In her scientist's logbook, she humorously recorded that — unlike more elusive computer bugs — this was the "first actual case of bug being found."
